How to reach the police:

  1. Find an Adult: If you need help, find a trusted adult, like a parent, teacher, or neighbor.
  2. Call 911: If it’s an emergency (like if someone is hurt or in danger), use a phone to call 911.

What to say:

  • Your name: "My name is[your name]."
  • Where you are: "I am at [your location]."
  • What's happening: "I need help because [explain the situation]."

How to act around police officers:

When you're lost:

  • Stay calm and look for a police officer.
  • Tell them your name, address, and parent's phone number.
  • Stay with the officer until your parents arrive.

When you see an accident:

  • Stay Safe: Move to a safe spot away from the accident.
  • Call for Help: Dial 911 or ask an adult to call for help.
  • Provide Information: Tell the operator where you are, what happened, and if anyone is hurt.
  • Wait: Stay on the line until the operator tells you it’s okay to hang up...

If an officer talks to you:

  • Stay Calm and Polite: Always be respectful and listen carefully to what the officer is saying.
  • Answer Questions: Answer their questions honestly and clearly. It's okay to ask if you don’t understand something.
  • Stay Still: Keep your hands where the officer can see them and don’t make sudden movements.

In an Emergency:

  • Call 911 if you need help right away.
  • Tell them your name, location, and what’s happening.
  • Follow their instructions.

If you get stopped:

  • Stay where you are.
  • Keep your hands where the officer can see them.
  • Stay Calm: Even if you’re nervous, try to stay calm and follow their instructions
