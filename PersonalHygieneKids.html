Personal Hygiene for Kids

Wash Your Hands:

  • When: Before eating, after using the bathroom, and when you touch something dirty.
  • How: Use soap and water, scrub for 20 seconds (sing "Happy Birthday" twice), then rinse and dry.

Brush Your Teeth:

  • When: Twice a day, in the morning and before bed.
  • How: Use a pea-sized amount of toothpaste, brush for 2 minutes (make sure to get all sides), then spit and rinse.

Floss Your Teeth:

  • When: Once a day, preferably at night.
  • How: Use a piece of floss, gently slide it between your teeth, and curve it around each tooth to remove food and plaque.

Take a Bath or Shower:

  • When: Every day or every other day.
  • How: Use soap to wash your whole body and shampoo to clean your hair. Rinse off all the soap and shampoo.

Wear Clean Clothes:

  • When: Every day, especially clean socks and underwear.
  • Why: Dirty clothes can have germs and make you smell bad.

Foot Care:

  • When: Wash your feet daily and wear clean socks.
  • Why: to prevent smelly feet.

Cover Your Mouth:

  • When: When you sneeze or cough.
  • How: Use a tissue or your elbow to cover your mouth and nose. Throw the tissue away and wash your hands.

Wash Your Hair:

  • When: 2-3 times a week.
  • How: Use shampoo to clean your hair, then rinse well. Use conditioner if needed and rinse it out too.
